1. Small-molecule modulation of β-arrestins

⭐ 👍 👎 已读
Alem W. Kahsai、Natalia Pakharukova、Henry Y. Kwon、Kunal S. Shah 等 35 人 · DOI: 10.1038/s41586-026-10683-5
一句话

该论文围绕β-arrestins这一GPCR信号调控核心蛋白，报道了可直接作用于β-arrestin的小分子抑制剂，并用药理、生化、生物物理与结构手段解析其如何阻断β-arrestin与激动剂激活GPCR的结合。摘要显示，代表性化合物Cmpd-5结合β-arrestin1中央crest区域的一个由middle、C和lariat loops构成的口袋，从而抑制去敏化、内吞及β-arrestin依赖生理功能，同时尽量不影响G蛋白-受体偶联。

贡献

1. 在靶点层面，提出并初步验证了“直接药理调控β-arrestin”这一此前缺少化学工具的策略，补上了GPCR药理学中相对空白的一环。2. 在分子机制层面，给出一个较具体的结合位点与作用模型：Cmpd-5作用于β-arrestin1中央crest、涉及middle loop、C loop与lariat loop形成的受体结合界面相关口袋，并稳定一种不利于受体结合的构象。3. 在功能层面，摘要指向较清晰的表型链条：抑制β-arrestin招募/结合，进而削弱受体去敏化、内吞和β-arrestin依赖的生理输出，但保留G protein–receptor coupling，提示其可能作为“选择性切断arrestin支路”的化学探针。4. 在方法整合层面，结合cryo-EM、分子动力学模拟和结构指导突变，说明作者不仅筛到活性分子，还试图把化合物-构象-功能之间的因果关系落到结构基础上。

为什么读

1. 对GPCR信号偏向性、受体去敏化、内吞和β-arrestin支路感兴趣的研究者，这篇论文值得读，因为它不是继续在受体正构/别构位点上做文章，而是把调控层级下移到β-arrestin本体，可能改变设计偏向性药物或化学探针的思路。2. 对做结构生物学和机制药理的人，文中最有吸引力的是“具体口袋+具体环区+具体构象效应”的描述：如果这一位点具有可重复利用性，那么β-arrestin可能成为可成药的蛋白-蛋白相互作用调控靶点。3. 对关注工具化合物的人，摘要所述‘保留G蛋白偶联、抑制arrestin相关功能’意味着这些分子有潜力作为区分两条信号支路的实验工具，用于在细胞和生理模型中拆解β-arrestin依赖表型。4. 来源为Nature，且从摘要看证据链覆盖药理、结构与模拟，说明其可能对领域内“是否能直接小分子调β-arrestin”这一长期问题提供较强支持，具有较高跟进价值。

注意点

1. 仅根据摘要，无法判断抑制剂的化学系列数量、活性范围、选择性、构效关系和可优化空间；“small-molecule inhibitors”可能不止一个，但具体命中质量未知。2. 摘要明确点名的是β-arrestin1与Cmpd-5，尚不清楚对β-arrestin2是否同样有效、是否存在亚型选择性，或这种选择性是否会限制泛化。3. “sparing G protein–receptor coupling”来自摘要表述，但不知道是在多少种GPCR、多少种细胞背景和多大剂量窗口下成立；潜在的受体类型依赖性和系统偏差无法从现有信息判断。4. 摘要提到“β-arrestin-dependent physiological functions”，但未给出具体生理模型、终点指标和效应大小，因此对体内可转化性、药代性质、安全性与脱靶风险目前不能做可靠判断。5. 由于摘要被截断，后半段关于构象机制、实验体系或更多关键结果缺失；因此不能据此断言其是否已证明广谱适用性、是否实现真正意义上的高选择性，或是否适合作为先导药物，只能将其视为很有前景的机制与工具化合物研究。

Abstract β-Arrestins are multifunctional regulators of G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) signalling and orchestrate diverse downstream signalling events and physiological responses across the GPCR superfamily 1–3 . Although GPCR pharmacology has advanced to target orthosteric and allosteric sites, as well as G proteins and GPCR kinases, direct chemical tools to modulate β-arrestin activities have remained conspicuously absent. Here we report the identification of small-molecule inhibitors that selectively target β-arrestins and delineate their mechanism of action through integrated pharmacological, biochemical, biophysical and structural analyses. These inhibitors disrupt β-arrestin engagement with agonist-activated GPCRs, impairing desensitization, internalization and β-arrestin-dependent physiological functions while sparing G protein–receptor coupling. Cryo-electron microscopy, molecular dynamics simulations and structure-guided mutagenesis reveal that one modulator, Cmpd-5, engages a pocket within the central crest of β-arrestin1 formed by the middle, C and lariat loops, a critical receptor-binding interface, stabilizing a distinct conformation that is incompatible with full β-arrestin–receptor engagement. Together, these findings establish a mechanistic framework for β-arrestin modulation, reveal a novel allosteric site for structure-based drug design, and open new avenues for transducer-targeted, pathway-specific GPCR therapeutic agents.

The identification of the cancer cell of origin is a fundamental question in cancer biology. We used fluorescent lineage tracing of independent mouse skin stem cell populations, single cell transcriptomics, and Duplex sequencing, to identify the origin of chemically induced skin tumors. Tumors arose predominantly from Lgr6+ and / or Lrig1+ stem cells of the upper hair follicle, but only very rarely from the Lgr5 + and Krt19 + hair follicle bulge. Lgr6 + stem cells initiated by dimethylbenzanthracene responded to tumor promoter treatment resulting in clonal expansion of initiated cells carrying the canonical Hras Q61L mutation. Spontaneous mutations in Kras also clonally expanded, but did not generate tumors unless the Hras gene was deleted, thus revealing a competitive interaction between Hras and Kras pathways that influences clonal selection.
